COVID-19 Charts & Timeline

All charts are built from peer-reviewed studies, CDC/NCHS mortality data, WHO reports, and FDA/EMA safety reviews. Sources are cited beneath each chart. The timeline covers November 2019 through 2025 — filterable by category.

01

COVID-19 Deaths

US & Global counts, excess mortality, and data problems
CDC / NCHS · Annual
US COVID-19 Deaths by Year
Official death certificate counts — COVID-19 as underlying or contributing cause (ICD-10 U07.1)
Sources: MMWR 2021 · MMWR 2022 · CDC/NCHS
WHO Global Estimate · 2022
Official COVID Deaths vs. Estimated Excess Deaths (Global, 2020–2021)
WHO estimated global excess deaths were 2.74× higher than officially reported COVID deaths — suggesting massive global undercounting
Source: Hong Kong excess mortality study citing WHO, 2024
MMWR 2022 · Death Certificate Data
COVID-19 Role in Deaths: Underlying vs. Contributing Cause (2022)
In 2022, COVID was the underlying cause (trigger) in 76% of cases — listed as contributing (additional) cause in 24%. Only 3.6% listed COVID as the sole cause.
Source: MMWR, COVID-19 Mortality Update 2022
US Excess Mortality Analysis · 2024
US Non-COVID Excess Deaths by Category (2020–2024)
Beyond direct COVID deaths, an estimated 280,000+ additional Americans died of indirect pandemic causes — delayed care, overwhelmed hospitals, and social disruption
Source: Cause-Specific Excess Mortality, US, 2024
02

How Deadly Was COVID-19?

Infection Fatality Rate (IFR) by age, global IFR distribution, comparisons
Ioannidis et al. 2022 · The Lancet 2022
Infection Fatality Rate (IFR) by Age Group — Pre-Vaccine Era
IFR rises exponentially with age. Risk for under-30s was comparable to seasonal flu. Risk for 80+ exceeded 10% in high-income countries.
Sources: Ioannidis et al., NCBI 2022 · Lancet IFR Analysis 2022
Context Comparison
COVID-19 vs. Other Mortality Events — US Annual Deaths
COVID-19 became the 3rd leading cause of US death in 2020 and 2021 — above stroke, Alzheimer's, diabetes, and flu
Sources: MMWR 2021 · CDC leading causes of death statistics
Pre-Vaccine Era · Multiple Studies
IFR Estimates Across Studies — Comparison (Overall Population, High-Income Countries)
Multiple independent research groups produced similar IFR estimates. Early 2020 estimates (high) were revised downward as seroprevalence studies showed more infections than confirmed.
Sources: Ioannidis 2022 · Lancet 2022 · NYC seroprevalence study · Wilson 2022 (CDC serology surveys)

Vaccine Efficacy Data

Clinical trial results, real-world effectiveness, waning immunity
Phase 3 RCT Results · 2020–2021
COVID-19 Vaccine Efficacy — Original Strain Clinical Trials
Efficacy against symptomatic infection in randomized controlled trials. Pfizer and Moderna led; other platforms showed lower but significant efficacy.
Source: COVID-19 VE Review, NCBI 2022
Real-World Effectiveness · vs. Variants
Pfizer (BNT162b2) Effectiveness by Variant — Symptomatic Infection
Real-world effectiveness dropped significantly against each new variant, particularly Omicron. Protection against severe disease remained higher than against infection.
Source: WHO rapid review, 2021 · Multiple VE studies
Booster Uptake Context
US COVID-19 Booster Authorization Timeline — Expanding Eligibility 2021–2024
Number of US population groups newly eligible for each booster authorization. Each expansion broadened who could get a booster dose.
Source: Immunize.org vaccine timeline · CDC Museum COVID-19 Timeline
04

Vaccine Adverse Events

Confirmed harms, rates, and comparison to COVID infection risk
VAERS note: VAERS death and adverse event reports are not confirmed causal deaths. They are passive reports filed by anyone and are used for signal detection only. Active surveillance (Vaccine Safety Datalink, clinical studies) provides causal evidence.
VAERS Through June 2023 · Signal Data
VAERS COVID-19 Vaccine Adverse Event Reports by Type
Total reports filed in VAERS. These are not confirmed as caused by vaccines — they are reports of events that occurred after vaccination and require further investigation.
Source: Autopsy findings in fatal vaccine-induced myocarditis, NCBI 2023
FDA / VSD / Israel Data · Confirmed Signal
Myocarditis Rate per 100,000 Doses — mRNA Vaccines in Males by Age
Rates from the Vaccine Safety Datalink (active surveillance) — more accurate than VAERS passive reporting. Risk highest in males 16–29 after dose 2.
Source: Myocarditis in young males, NCBI 2023 · MMWR Oregon study 2024
Risk Comparison · COVID vs. Vaccine
Myocarditis: COVID Infection vs. Vaccination (per 100,000)
COVID-19 infection causes far more myocarditis than the vaccines — approximately 150 cases per 100,000 vs. 2–15 from mRNA vaccination. Context essential for risk evaluation.
Source: National Academies evidence review, 2024 · CDC Boehmer et al. 2021
Confirmed Causal Adverse Events · EMA / FDA
Serious Confirmed Vaccine Adverse Events — Estimated Rates per Million Doses
Rates for adverse events where a causal relationship has been officially confirmed. All are rare; rates vary significantly by vaccine type.
Sources: VITT review, NCBI 2023 · WHO AEFI surveillance 2023 · FDA 2024
05

Mask Efficacy Data

RCT evidence vs. observational studies vs. lab data
Cochrane Review 2023 · 78 RCTs
Evidence Quality Rating by Mask Study Type
The Cochrane Review methodology accepts only RCTs. Observational studies have higher infection risk but are excluded from its conclusions due to high confounding.
Sources: Jefferson et al., Cochrane 2023 · Systematic review, NCBI 2023
RCT Meta-Analysis · NCBI 2023
Mask Type Effectiveness in RCTs (Odds Ratio — lower = more protective)
N95 respirators showed statistically significant protection (OR 0.03). Surgical masks showed little difference from controls in RCTs. Observational data tells a different story.
Source: RCT meta-analysis, NCBI 2023

Hospital & ICU Strain

Capacity data from HHS Protect federal database and peer-reviewed studies
HHS Protect Data · NCBI/PMC 2022
US Hospital ICU Capacity Status During Winter Wave (Sept 2020 – Feb 2021)
Share of 1,753 acute care hospitals by ICU capacity status during the third COVID wave — the worst for hospital strain
Source: Figueroa et al., NCBI/PMC 2022
ICU Model · CDC MMWR 2021
Predicted Additional Excess Deaths by ICU Occupancy Level
CDC-backed statistical model linking hospital ICU strain to additional excess deaths 2 weeks later. Hospital collapse had a measurable mortality multiplier effect.
Source: MMWR: Hospital Strain & Excess Deaths, 2021
Survey of 625 US Hospitals · NCBI 2022
Hospital Alert Rates for Overcrowding (March 2020 – April 2021)
Percentage of participating hospitals that reported at least one alert for the listed resource strain during the study period
Source: ICU Overcrowding & Ventilator Shortages, NCBI 2022
